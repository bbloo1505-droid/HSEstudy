# Study primer: Queensland WHS legal framework

[[page:primer-1]]

## Purpose

This is a **general explanatory** study digest for HSE Launchpad. It is not legislation and must not be cited as an official duty. Replace it with text extracted from the Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (Qld) and Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011 (Qld).

## How the framework fits together

[[page:primer-2]]

1. **WHS Act** — establishes duties and legal principles (for example, primary duty of care concepts, consultation principles, and the idea of what is reasonably practicable).
2. **WHS Regulation** — sets detailed legal requirements that expand on the Act.
3. **Approved codes of practice** — practical guidance for meeting duties. Codes are not a substitute for reading the Act and Regulation, but following an approved code can help demonstrate what was reasonably practicable in many situations.
4. **Australian Standards and technical methods** — measurement and technical procedures used when assessing or controlling hazards.
5. **Company procedures** — approved internal operating methods. Follow them after induction; they do not override the law.

## Key duty concepts to learn (structure, not section memorisation)

[[page:primer-3]]

- **PCBU** — person conducting a business or undertaking; holds primary duties to ensure health and safety so far as is reasonably practicable.
- **Officer due diligence** — officers must exercise due diligence to ensure the PCBU complies.
- **Worker duties** — take reasonable care, comply with reasonable instructions and policies.
- **Consultation** — workers must be consulted on WHS matters that affect them.
- **Reasonably practicable** — weigh likelihood, degree of harm, knowledge, availability of ways to eliminate or minimise risk, and cost.
- **Hierarchy of controls** — eliminate, substitute, isolate, engineering, administrative, PPE (PPE is not first-line preference).
- **Competent person** — someone with knowledge, experience and (where required) qualifications for the task.

## Consultant relevance

As an Associate HSE Consultant you must know *where* a requirement lives (Act / Regulation / code / standard / procedure) and when to escalate. Do not invent section numbers. Open the official document and verify with your supervisor.
