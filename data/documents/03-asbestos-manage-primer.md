# Study primer: Asbestos management

[[page:asb-1]]

## Terminology

- **ACM** — asbestos-containing material.
- **Friable** — material that can be crumbled, pulverised or reduced to powder by hand pressure when dry; higher airborne fibre potential when disturbed.
- **Non-friable (bonded)** — asbestos fibres bound in a matrix (for example cement sheet); risk rises when cut, abraded or weathered.
- **PACM** — presumed asbestos-containing material where sampling has not confirmed status but presence is reasonably assumed based on age/product type.

Queensland currently uses the *How to manage and control asbestos in the workplace Code of Practice 2021* and the *How to safely remove asbestos Code of Practice 2021*, both varied in January 2023. Confirm currency before relying on any extract.

## Registers and management plans

[[page:asb-2]]

An **asbestos register** records identified (and often presumed) ACM, locations, condition and related information used to manage risk. An **asbestos management plan** describes how asbestos will be managed at the workplace. Incomplete registers (missing areas, no photos, outdated condition notes) are a common consulting finding — treat them as incomplete, not as clearance.

## Survey purposes

[[page:asb-3]]

- **Management survey** — support ongoing management of ACM in occupied buildings.
- **Refurbishment / demolition survey** — more intrusive investigation before works that may disturb fabric.

Sampling has limitations: inaccessible voids, representative sampling uncertainty, and laboratory method limits. Never declare a material asbestos-containing from a photograph alone.

## Roles

[[page:asb-4]]

Understand the difference between consultant advice, licensed assessor functions (where applicable), and licensed removalists. Friable removal requires a Class A licence. Removal of more than 10 m² of non-friable asbestos requires an appropriately licensed business. Confirm licensing details in the current Regulation/code.
